How the Salas 64 Rankings Work

A blended system combining résumé, efficiency, roster health, and March upside.
← Back to rankings

1. Results & Résumé (35–45%)

  • Quality wins/losses by opponent strength (KenPom/EvanMiya tiers) and game site (home/away/neutral).
  • Margin with diminishing returns (garbage-time discount).
  • Schedule strength to date and balance of true road/neutral results.
  • Consistency: avoiding bad losses, week-over-week stability.

2. Team Quality & Efficiency (30–40%)

  • KenPom: AdjO, AdjD, overall efficiency margin, tempo context.
  • EvanMiya: BPR/BPM-style team quality, lineup RAPM/“EVD”, on/off impact.
  • Four-factor profile (eFG%, TO%, ORB%, FTR) and shot profile (rim/3, transition).
  • Rebounding (both ends), turnover creation, free-throw rate and opponent FTA.
  • Pace-adjusted dominance: performance vs. top-50/top-100 efficiency teams.

3. Roster & Availability (10–15%)

  • Current injuries/suspensions/returns (Covers injury report) with role-weighted impact (starters > rotation > depth).
  • Lineup continuity, guard play stability (PG/primary creator), rim protection and spacing.
  • Seniors/experience and usage balance (one-man reliance penalty).

4. Context & March Outlook (5–10%)

  • Neutral-court readiness, close-game execution (late-game turnover/rebound/FT).
  • Coaching track record for adjustments, defensive floor, travel/altitude factors when relevant.
  • Trending form (last 2–4 games) with small decay toward older games.

5. Mid-Major Consideration (explicit check)

  • Automatic-bid likelihood, top-100 efficiency viability, veteran guard play, and upset tools (threes volume + rim defense).
  • Résumé protection: strong road/neutral results can lift them 3–8 spots vs. power peers with similar metrics.

6. Tie-Breakers (in order)

  • Head-to-head (if reasonably comparable contexts).
  • Better neutral/road résumé.
  • Higher defensive floor (lower AdjD).
  • Healthier current roster (key players active).
  • Stronger trend over the past week.
